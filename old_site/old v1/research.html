Max Nathan LSE

I’m an economic geographer who uses a lot of economics.

I have three main research areas:

  1. The economics of cultural diversity – in particular, the economic performance of diverse cities, and links between immigration, diverse communities and innovation. This is the focus of my PHD.

  2. Innovation systems – I’m looking at both the globalisation of innovative activity, and the continuing importance of proximity in supporting ideas creation and flow.

  3. Public policy for cities – drawing on my policy background, I’m interested in urban economic development strategies, and optimal forms of urban governance.

I’m also interested in cities and the green economy, the creative industries and in policies for urban public space.
